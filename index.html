Heterogeneity-Adjusted EWAS Explorer

A web resource for exploring heterogeneity-adjusted EWAS results and methylation heterogeneity signals inferred from standard DNA methylation arrays. The haEWAS-Explorer supports phenotype-level summaries, associated-loci browsing, data download and matrix conversion for user-provided EPIC/450K methylation data.

Start Exploring Get Data
  • Association Summary: Provides an overview of significant loci identified by conventional EWAS, haEWAS and their overlap across curated phenotypes and methylation metrics.
  • Associated Loci Browser: Supports phenotype-specific exploration of associated loci through Manhattan plots, searchable result tables, metric-based filtering and CSV download.
  • A2H Matrix Conversion: Converts EPIC/450K array-based average methylation matrices into CpG-level CAMDA and CHALM values using A2H models, enabling users to perform heterogeneity-adjusted EWAS on their own datasets.

Why Heterogeneity-Adjusted EWAS (haEWAS)?

DNA methylation is commonly summarized as average methylation, defined as the fraction of DNA molecules methylated at a CpG in a bulk sample. Although useful, average methylation is incomplete. The same average methylation level can arise from distinct underlying methylation architectures: methylation may be concentrated in a subset of DNA molecules, alleles or cellular subpopulations, or distributed broadly across the sample. Methylation heterogeneity captures this distributional information, which conventional array-based EWAS based on β-values cannot directly resolve.

Sequencing-based approaches can quantify methylation heterogeneity from read-level methylation patterns, but such data are unavailable for most large human cohorts. haEWAS addresses this barrier by learning the relationship between average methylation and heterogeneity-aware methylation measures from deep reference methylomes and transferring this information to standard EPIC/450K methylation array datasets using CpG-specific average-to-heterogeneity conversion models, referred to as A2H. This enables re-analysis of existing array cohorts without requiring new sequencing experiments. In this resource, heterogeneity-aware measures include CAMDA and CHALM.

The haEWAS-Explorer provides access to curated population-scale haEWAS results across human phenotypes. Across these analyses, haEWAS identifies phenotype-associated loci missed by conventional EWAS and supports interactive visualization, filtering, associated-loci inspection and CSV download of harmonized statistics for β-value, CAMDA and CHALM analyses. The resource also includes an online matrix converter for applying A2H models to user-provided EPIC/450K methylation array data.
