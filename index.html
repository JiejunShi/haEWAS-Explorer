Heterogeneity-Adjusted EWAS Explorer

A web resource for heterogeneity-adjusted epigenome-wide association studies. The platform applies a CpG-specific Methylation Average-to-Heterogeneity Conversion Model Set (A2H) to derive CpG-specific estimates of methylation heterogeneity (hm) from array-measured average methylation (m̄), enabling identification of phenotype-associated loci not detected by conventional EWAS.

Why Heterogeneity-Adjusted EWAS (haEWAS)?

Epigenome-wide association studies (EWAS) have identified many DNA methylation changes associated with disease and complex traits. Most EWAS analyse microarray-derived beta values, which summarize average methylation (m̄) across bulk samples. Average methylation, however, cannot resolve differences in the distribution of methylation states across cells or DNA molecules within bulk samples. This distributional variation is referred to here as methylation heterogeneity, denoted hm. Cell-type deconvolution addresses compositional variation, but not heterogeneity within the underlying methylation patterns. Direct measurement of hm by whole-genome bisulfite sequencing (WGBS) remains impractical for many large cohorts.

haEWAS addresses this limitation by inferring hm from standard methylation array signals. Using a CpG-specific Methylation Average-to-Heterogeneity Conversion Model Set, hereafter referred to as A2H, the framework links m̄ to measures such as CAMDA and CHALM, the framework extends EWAS from m̄-based association to hm-aware association without requiring sequencing-based assays. This enables re-analysis of existing array cohorts to detect phenotype-associated epigenetic variation that may be missed by conventional EWAS.

This resource provides curated haEWAS meta-analysis results across phenotypes, tissues, and analytical settings, with harmonized statistics for Beta, CAMDA, and CHALM metrics. It also supports application of A2H to user-provided array methylation data. Use Explore for interactive filtering and locus-level inspection, and Download for reproducible CSV exports.
