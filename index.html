Why Heterogeneity-Adjusted EWAS (haEWAS)?

Epigenome-wide association studies (EWAS) have identified many DNA methylation changes associated with disease and complex traits. Most EWAS analyse microarray-derived beta values, which summarize mean methylation across bulk samples. This summary can obscure differences in single-molecule methylation heterogeneity between loci with similar mean levels. Cell-type deconvolution addresses compositional variation, but not heterogeneity within the underlying methylation patterns. Direct measurement of such heterogeneity by whole-genome bisulfite sequencing (WGBS) remains impractical for many large cohorts.

haEWAS addresses this limitation by inferring heterogeneity-related metrics from standard methylation array signals. By linking mean methylation levels to measures such as CHALM and CAMDA, the framework extends EWAS from average-level association to heterogeneity-aware association without requiring sequencing-based assays. This enables re-analysis of existing array cohorts to detect phenotype-associated epigenetic variation that may be missed by conventional EWAS.

This resource provides curated haEWAS meta-analysis results across phenotypes, tissues, and analytical settings, with harmonized statistics for Beta, CHALM, and CAMDA metrics. Use Explore for interactive filtering and locus-level inspection, and Download for reproducible CSV exports.

Note: CHALM quantifies promoter methylation as the proportion of reads containing at least one methylated CpG within a promoter region. CAMDA quantifies the co-occurrence of active methylation and demethylation signals within individual bisulfite sequencing reads.
